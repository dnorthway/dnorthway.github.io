Tutorial - Getting Started with the ProjectProdigy Project Management App for Excel on the Web

Welcome to the ProjectProdigy Project Management App Tutorial!

In this guide, we'll walk you through the features and steps to make the most out of this powerful Excel on the web application designed for General Contractors, Specialty Contractors, Suppliers, and Developers.

Synopsis:

The ProjectProdigy app is a JavaScript Excel API application that seamlessly integrates with Excel on the web, utilizing platforms like OneDrive and Dropbox to streamline your project management tasks. It automates the creation of essential post-award project documents, making your workflow more efficient. With modules for Purchase Orders, Change Orders, RFIs, RFPs, and Transmittals/Submittals, this app is your go-to tool for staying organized and enhancing collaboration.

Advantages:

  • Automation: ProjectProdigy automates the creation, update, and maintenance of various project management documents, saving you time and reducing manual effort.
  • Easy Data Management: Create, update, filter, and sort records effortlessly. Generate Purchase Orders, RFIs, RFPs, Change Orders, and Transmittals/Submittals with ease.
  • Seamless Contact Integration: Paste your Outlook project contacts directly into the app. No need to manually input contact information, enhancing accuracy and saving time.
  • Organized Dashboard: The intuitive Dashboard offers quick navigation and filters for easy access to project information at a glance.
  • Cloud Storage Integration: Attachments can be stored on OneDrive, Dropbox, and more. Say goodbye to large email attachments; simply share a hyperlink to access files.
  • Platform Flexibility: Access the app via a web browser on Windows, Mac, and iPad. No need for Microsoft 365 or Office software.

Getting Started:

  1. Installation: Right click and save the provided manifest as a .xml file to your computer. Upload the Project Template to your OneDrive and open it in Excel on the web. Select Add-ins, select Manage My Add-ins, select Upload My Add-in then upload the 'manifest.xml' file to install the app. Click the ProjectProdigy App Save Document button, the task pane will appear.
  2. Contacts: Begin by entering or pasting your Outlook project-specific contacts. These could include A/E, owner, subcontractors, and vendors. Access further help by clicking >HELP on the contacts sheet.
  3. Purchase Order: Create Purchase Orders by filling in the shaded fields. Click the ProjectProdigy App Save Document button, and in the task pane, click the Save Purchase Order button. You can view and print POs and their respective logs.
  4. Subcontract Change Order: Similar to Purchase Orders, enter or select information in shaded fields, save with the ProjectProdigy App, and view/print SCCOs and their logs.
  5. Requests for Proposal (RFP): Fill in the shaded fields, delete unused cells, save with the app, and view/print RFPs, their logs, summaries, proposals, and budget effects.
  6. Requests for Information (RFI): Enter/select information, save, view, and print RFIs and their logs. You can also update RFIs by selecting the appropriate row and filling in unshaded cells.
  7. Transmittals/Submittals: Select the specification section and provide relevant information. Delete or add descriptions as needed, save, view, and print transmittals and their logs. Update by clicking >UpdateTransmittal on the TransmittalView sheet.

With ProjectProdigy, your project management becomes efficient and organized. Say goodbye to manual document creation and welcome automated excellence. Happy managing!

Remember, this tutorial is just a starting point. Dive into the app, explore its features, and tailor them to your specific project management needs.
